The Milky Way

Home among 400 billion suns

Spiral Arms

Four major arms spiral outward from the galactic core: Perseus, Sagittarius, Scutum-Centaurus, and our own Orion Arm. Each arm is a wave of density, compressing gas and dust, igniting new stars as it passes through the disk.

The Galactic Core

At the heart lies Sagittarius A*, a supermassive black hole four million times our Sun's mass. Stars orbit it at incredible speeds, their paths revealing the invisible giant that anchors our galaxy's slow, majestic rotation.

Our Place

We orbit 26,000 light-years from the center, taking 230 million years to complete one galactic year. The last time we were here, dinosaurs had just appeared on Earth. In our cosmic neighborhood, familiar constellations are merely local neighbors.

The Cosmic Ocean

The Milky Way is just one island in a universe of two trillion galaxies. We drift through the Local Group, bound to Andromeda, destined to merge in four billion years into a new spiral we may one day call Milkomeda.
