Beyond Light

At the event horizon, spacetime curves so severely that all paths lead inward. Light itself, trapped in this cosmic whirlpool, spirals endlessly around the darkness, creating the luminous photon ring that marks the boundary between our universe and the unknown.

Time Dilation

To a distant observer, time near the black hole slows to a crawl. An astronaut falling through the horizon would appear frozen at the edge, their image redshifting until they fade from view, while they themselves experience only moments.

The Singularity

At the heart lies a point of infinite density where our physics breaks down. The singularity is not a place in space but a moment in time, the inevitable future of anything that crosses the threshold into darkness.
