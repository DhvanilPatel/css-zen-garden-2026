The Ringed Giant

A billion ice crystals dancing in orbit

Celestial Wonder

Imagine standing on one of the sixty-two moons, watching the rings stretch across your entire sky. Each ring is not solid but composed of countless particles, from tiny ice crystals to boulders the size of mountains, all held in delicate gravitational ballet.

Ancient Light

The rings may be young in cosmic terms, perhaps only 100 million years old. Some celestial event, a moon torn apart by tidal forces or a comet captured and shredded, gave birth to this magnificent structure we now observe from across the solar system.

Shepherd Moons

Tiny moons orbit within the rings themselves, their gravity sculpting gaps and waves in the particle streams. These shepherds keep order in the chaos, their paths carved over millennia into perfect resonance with their icy charges.
