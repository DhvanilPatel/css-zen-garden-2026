When Winter Whispers

A meditation on silence and snow

In the hush before dawn breaks,

the first flake descends—

a single crystal traveler

from clouds that never end.

It carries winter's secrets,

whispered from the sky,

each frozen fractal pattern

a geometry of goodbye.

The pines stand sentinel,

cloaked in robes of white,

guardians of the quiet

through the longest night.

And we who watch the falling,

wrapped in wool and wonder,

know that beauty often comes

when the world slows down to slumber.

— From the journal of Elara Northwood, Winter Solstice, Year of the Silent Snow
