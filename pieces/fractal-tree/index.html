Recursive Growth

Each branch contains the whole

"The part contains the pattern of the whole. Break a branch from a tree, and you hold a smaller tree in your hands." — Benoit Mandelbrot

The Algorithm of Life

Nature discovered fractals long before mathematics gave them a name. Every tree follows the same simple rule: divide, angle, repeat. From trunk to twig, the pattern persists.

Scale Invariance

Zoom into a branch. You find smaller branches. Zoom again. The pattern continues. At what point does branch become twig? The boundary dissolves under examination.

Infinite Complexity

A finite rule generates infinite detail. This is the magic of recursion: simplicity breeding complexity, order emerging from repetition.
